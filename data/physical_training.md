## 1. Periodization: The "Norwegian Method" for Tennis

The core objective is to maximize "Metabolic Efficiency" and "Explosive Repeatability." For tennis, this translates to maintaining high-intensity output (0-4 shot rallies) across longer sets (50+ points) without systemic failure.

### Incremental Load & Intensity Blocks
*   **Volume over Intensity:** Focus on high-volume, low-intensity (V2) aerobic base to build a "metabolic engine" that clears lactate faster.
*   **Double-Threshold Days (Simulated):** 
    *   *Morning:* Tactical court session (Lactate <2.5 mmol/L).
    *   *Afternoon:* Interval-based physical session (Lactate 3.0–4.0 mmol/L).
*   **Micro-Periodization:** 3-week "Load" cycles followed by 1-week "Adaptation/Deload" cycles.

### Intensity Zones (Tennis Specific)
| Zone | Metabolic State | Training Type | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Zone 1** | Recovery | Active mobility, light jogging. | Lactate clearing, psychological reset. |
| **Zone 2** | Aerobic Base | Steady-state cardio, technical drills. | Mitochondrial density, fat oxidation. |
| **Zone 3** | Threshold | Long-rally drills, sustained movement. | Lactate threshold (The "Challenger Grinder" zone). |
| **Zone 4** | Anaerobic | 0-4 shot explosive patterns, S+1 drills. | VO2 Max, explosive power repeatability. |

## 2. Monitoring Metrics: Risk vs. Reward

Precision monitoring prevents overtraining and identifies the "Sweet Spot" for adaptation.

### Objective Metrics
*   **HRV (Heart Rate Variability):**
    *   *Target:* Baseline ±10%.
    *   *Warning:* >15% drop below baseline = Reduce intensity or switch to Zone 1.
*   **ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio):**
    *   *Calculation:* (Workload current 7 days) / (Avg workload last 28 days).
    *   *The Sweet Spot:* **0.8 – 1.3**.
    *   *Danger Zone:* >1.5 (High injury risk).
*   **Hydration/Mass:**
    *   Urine Color 1–3 (Hydrated).
    *   Body Mass Change <2% post-session.

### Subjective Metrics
*   **sRPE (Session-RPE):** `RPE (1-10) x Duration (mins)`.
    *   Ex: A 90-min "Heavy" session (RPE 8) = 720 units.
    *   *Target:* Maintain consistent weekly sRPE totals to stabilize ACWR.

## 4. Recovery & Maintenance Protocols

"Training is only as effective as the recovery that follows it."

### Sleep Architecture
*   **Target:** 8.5 – 9.5 hours per night.
*   **Consistency:** Wake/Sleep window within ±30 mins daily.
*   **Protocol:** No screens 60 mins before sleep; cool room temp (18°C).

### Post-Training Nutrition (The 45-Min Window)
*   **Protein:** 25-30g High-quality protein (Whey/Leucine rich).
*   **Carbs:** 1.0–1.2g/kg body weight to replenish glycogen.
*   **Hydration:** 150% of weight lost during session (fluids + electrolytes).

### Mobility & Soft Tissue
*   **Daily:** 15-min dynamic flow (mornings).
*   **Post-Session:** Static stretching & foam rolling focused on posterior chain/hips.
*   **Weekly:** Deep tissue/physio session for injury screening.
